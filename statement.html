Statement

Here are statement of my research over the years and in the future, and where my interests and research locate in the big picture of TCS and learning theory. See Projects for more details of my research.

Generally

During my very first research about online optimization, I become curious about online decision making under uncertainty: how to make decision if algorithms know little about future? Going beyond this restriction of algorithms, I am gradually interested in algorithms with more restrictions such as restricted computing resources: what if algorithms don't have enough time, space or measurement capacity to deal with numerous input?

Since that, I am always been attracted by uncertain and numerous objects, where I am obsessed with designing efficient and elegant algorithms with theoretical guarantees, from a complexity and geometric perspective.

My interest of algorithms under uncertainty is mainly in decision making in online setting, to be specific, decision making in the face of an ever-changing or even adversarial environment. My current research is online decision making from lens of both online algorithms and online optimization, using tools from convex geometry.

My interest of algorithms with numerous input is mainly in sublinear algorithms, to be specific, streaming algorithms (sublinear space), property testing (sublinear time) and compressed sensing (sublinear measurement). I am also interested in developing matching lower bounds for these problems, which may rely on rather different techniques than the algorithms themselves, specifically, communication complexity. Where my research about this field lies is still under consideration.
